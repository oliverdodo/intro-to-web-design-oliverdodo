Growing up on the East Coast

Growing up on the East Coast gave me a perspective that I think a lot of kids lack here in San Diego. I grew up in Essex County, where I lived in a pretty nice area but what was interesting about that neighborhood layout was that there were a lot of poorer areas next to my house as well. As a result, a ton of my friends lived in those less nice areas, and I hung out there a lot even when my mom said it was unsafe. A core memory of mine was going to Giants games with my dad at Metlife Stadium. They never won a ton of games, but it was a core memory for me because my dad was a lifetime fan of the Jets and Giants ever since he moved to New York from Vietnam. As a kid I never really did appreciate New York since it was always so close to me, but now I wish I could go back and do a ton of stuff I never did growing up there. Don't get me wrong San Diego is great and I love the weather here, but something about a New York winter feels nostalgic and feels like home.

Building my Photography Business

When I played tee ball for my local recreation baseball league, there was this parent photographer with an expensive camera that would always take photos of our games. The best players on our team would always get most of the photos, and for everyone else they would be lucky to get 1 or 2. Now obviously nobody cared a ton because they were seven, but to me I always thought that it was kind of unfair even before I knew about photography. Fast forward to freshman year where I'd just bought my first mirrorless camera after months of using my dad's old DSLR. I knew I wanted to shoot mainly JV games, as that's where most of my friends were and varsity always got the majority of the media. I started out really bad at it, mainly because I was shy and didn't want to get in the way of things. But over time I developed treating photography as more of a business, learning about demand and how I could start making money. Now I'm shooting for multiple organizations not to mention CCA sports as well including lacrosse, wrestling, and more. Learn more about photography at Professional Photographers of America.

Watching Basketball

I started watching basketball when I moved to California. I never watched a ton of basketball when I lived in NY because all the teams sucked there, but when I moved my local team would be the greatest basketball franchise of all time: the Los Angeles Lakers. Around the time I moved it was around the playoffs, and the Lakers were a 7th seed and they made it all the way to the conference finals. That playoff run got me obsessed with the NBA, and made me a loyal Laker fan. The next two seasons weren't that great, both times we got eliminated in the first round. But one of the best days of my life was when Luka Doncic got traded to the Lakers, as now we have a star for the next ten years after LeBron retires. Me and my Dad have gone to at least one game per season, and we always enjoy the food and environment that Crypto Arena offers.
